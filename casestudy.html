Baker Buddies Case Study

Baker buddies originated from a collaborative web media project where the objective was to create an article list website of our choosing. One of the main goals of this project was to create not only a brand, but build a live website using html and css. Visit the baker buddies website.

Date of project:
September- November 2020
Collaborators:
Mikayla Green, Caroline Mooney and Jeremy Nolasco

What is Baker Buddies?

Baker Buddies is an interactive baking website that promotes simple and easy baking in a group setting. Each recipe is divided into different roles for every member of the group. It's a great way to connect with others while making something delicious! baker buddies can be used by anyone, but our main audience catered towards SUNY Oswego students who are looking for easy baking while being social with others.

My Contributions to Baker Buddies

  • Created our logo and color palette.
  • Started our brand guide which included our mission statement, color palette and typography.
  • Preliminary sketches of our design layout
  • Illustrated our 404 page animation
  • Created a CSS grid for tools required for each recipe
  • Started a layout on our recipe pages

Brand Identity

As a group of designers, we understand how important it is to create a brand that represents the aesthetic and goals of our website. Since the beginning, we wanted our audience to feel welcomed by our website so that they can engage with our recipes and create something memorable with their teammates. Baking should be positive experience and we wanted our website to send that message.

baker buddies is presented in all lowercase to make the audience relaxed and at ease while using the site. The curves in the slab serif show that baker buddies are fun and playful. In the middle of the typography there is a chef hat graphic to add visual interest to our logo. When users see a chef hat they will know that they have come to a baking site. Like our website, we wanted our color palette to be relaxed and playful at the same time. This is why our team opted for a muted color palette, with a pop of the red-orange as an accent color. This palette will not overwhelm users when they enter the site which is important so our team of bakers don't feel overwhelmed while in the kitchen.

Homepage

Our homepage contains an article list of our most recent recipes as well as the phrase, “designed for group baking” which can be seen throughout the website.

About

The about page explains the mission of baker buddies as well as the importance of working together and baking with others. On the left side of the page there is an image of two young adults to help connect to our primary audience, SUNY Oswego students.

Article List

Our recipe page contains an article list of all of our recipes. Staying on brand with our primary audience, all of our recipes are named after SUNY Oswego. In place of photographs of the deserts, we opted to use illustrations for the thumbnail so there would be unity among all of the imagery. Each article box animations to a dashed line when you hover over it as an interactive element to our website.

Individual Recipe Page

Each recipe page includes a description of the desert and an illustration. They are also provided with the ingredients, tools and directions they need to create the dish. To promote group baking, each teammate has a role so everyone is working. Images of the tools are provided in a grid format so that users can easily see what they need. Since the original recipe was not created by baker buddies, we credited the original baker at the bottom of the recipe, so they receive the proper credit.

404 Page

If the user is misdirected, they will receive our 404 page. Staying on brand, there is an animation of a oven on fire with text that reads, “Something went wrong in the kitchen”. There is a link at the bottom that redirects to the homepage.

Logo Animation

When hovering over the logo, the chef hat rotates as an interactive feature of our website.

Conclusion

This was the first website I have completed. As a new web designer, this project helped me learn many new skills both in html and CSS. I am very proud of the work my team and I have done to make baker buddies a fun experience for everyone.
